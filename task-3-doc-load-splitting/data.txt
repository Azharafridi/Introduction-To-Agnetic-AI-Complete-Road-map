The impact of climate change on freshwater resources is becoming increasingly evident across the globe. Rising temperatures, shifting precipitation patterns, and extreme weather events are altering the availability and quality of water supplies. 
 In many regions, prolonged droughts are reducing river flows, lowering reservoir levels, and depleting groundwater reserves. These changes pose serious threats to drinking water security, agriculture, and ecosystem health. 
 To adapt, countries and communities must invest in water-saving technologies, improve water governance, and enhance infrastructure for capturing and storing rainfall. Furthermore, international cooperation is needed to manage shared water basins and prevent conflict over scarce resources. 
 Innovative solutions such as desalination, wastewater recycling, and AI-powered irrigation systems can also play a key role in building climate-resilient water systems.
